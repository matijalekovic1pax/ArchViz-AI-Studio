First Steps

Getting Started

New to AVAS? This section walks you through your first generation in under 5 minutes.

No software needed. AVAS runs entirely in your browser. Sign in with your company account and you're ready to go — no downloads, no installations.

Your First Render

Log in at your AVAS URL using your company credentials.
Select a mode from the top bar. For your first try, choose 3D Rendering.
Upload an image by dragging it onto the central canvas, or clicking the upload area in the left sidebar. You can use a screenshot of a 3D model, a render, or a photo.
Optionally, pick a style in the left panel (e.g. "Contemporary Minimalist" or "Neo-Brutalist").
Press the Generate button (or ⌘ Enter) and wait — typically 10–30 seconds.
Your result appears on the canvas. Download it using the download button in the top-right corner.
Tip: You can always undo and redo using the arrows in the top bar, and your generation history is always visible in the bottom panel under the "History" tab.

Understanding Credits

AVAS uses a credit system. Each generation consumes a small number of credits. Higher quality settings and more complex modes cost slightly more.

  • Your remaining credits are shown in the top bar.
  • When you run low, you'll see a warning before generating.
  • Credits reset monthly according to your plan.
  • Batch operations (Upscale, PDF Compression) offer ~30% savings vs. individual processing.

The Interface

AVAS has a five-panel workspace. Here's what each part does.

🔝
Top Bar
Navigation & mode selection

The top bar lets you switch between all 17 modes, access the download menu, change the interface language, undo/redo recent actions, and manage your account.

Mode Selector Download Undo / Redo Language Account

Left Sidebar — Workflow
Mode-specific inputs and controls

The left sidebar changes based on the active mode. Here you upload images, configure workflow-specific settings, run AI analysis tools, and build up your generation inputs. Every mode has its own left panel with relevant controls.

🖼️
Central Canvas
Main viewport for images and video

The canvas displays your uploaded image and generated results. You can zoom using the scroll wheel, pan by holding Spacebar and dragging, and use the zoom controls in the corner. In Visual Edit mode, selection tools are drawn directly on the canvas.

Zoom Pan Split View (before/after) Selection Drawing

Right Panel — Settings
Output quality, style, and render parameters

The right panel gives you control over how the output is generated — choose your resolution, aspect ratio, quality level, style, lighting, camera, materials, and more. You don't need to touch all these settings; the defaults produce excellent results for most use cases.

🔽
Bottom Panel
Prompt, history, and tools

The bottom panel has several tabs depending on the mode:

  • Prompt — View and edit the AI prompt. AVAS auto-generates a prompt from your settings, but you can also type your own.
  • History — A thumbnail grid of all previous generations in this session. Click any to restore it.
  • Timeline (Video & Exploded) — Playback controls and keyframe editor.
  • Legend (Masterplan) — Zone color key.

3D Rendering

Most Used Requires uploaded image Up to 4K

Transform a 3D model screenshot — from Revit, Rhino, SketchUp, Blender, 3ds Max, ArchiCAD, Cinema 4D, or any other 3D software — into a photorealistic architectural render. AVAS analyzes your model and applies lighting, materials, atmosphere, and style based on your settings.

What to upload: A screenshot or exported image of your 3D model — it can be a shaded viewport, a clay render, or an existing render you want to improve. The higher quality your input, the better the output.

Step-by-Step

Upload your 3D model screenshot by dragging it onto the canvas.
Click Analyze Problem Areas in the left sidebar to let AVAS auto-detect complex zones (optional but recommended for detailed models).
Choose an architectural style from the style grid — hover over any style to preview it.
Optionally adjust rendering mode, lighting, camera, and resolution in the right panel.
Press Generate.

Rendering Modes

Enhance — Improves your model while maintaining photorealism. Best for clean 3D models.

Stylize — Applies a strong artistic interpretation. Great for concept presentations.

Hybrid — Balanced between realism and style. The most versatile option.

Strict Realism — Maximum photorealism, minimal artistic license. For client presentations.

Concept-Push — Strong conceptual interpretation. For early design stages.

Problem Area Analysis

AVAS can scan your uploaded image and automatically flag areas that might be challenging to render — such as thin elements, complex glass surfaces, or intricate details. This helps you decide where to apply extra care.

Structural issues Envelope complexity Interior zones Site elements Confidence scoring

Background Reference

Upload a photo of a real location or environment in the left sidebar. AVAS will use it as context for the background — placing your building in a specific urban or natural setting.

CAD to Render

Floor plans, elevations, sections Auto layer detection

Convert any architectural CAD drawing — floor plan, elevation, section, or site plan — into a photorealistic rendered visualization. AVAS auto-detects the layers in your drawing and uses them to generate accurate materials and spatial depth.

Supported input: Export your CAD file as a PNG or JPG (screenshot or rendered view) and upload it directly. Clean black-and-white line drawings produce the best results.

Key Settings

CAD Settings ▾
Drawing Type Select Plan, section, elevation, or site. Tells AVAS how to interpret the geometry.
Scale Select / Text 1:50 · 1:100 · 1:200 · 1:500 · 1:1000 · custom
Auto Layer Detection Button AI scans your drawing and categorizes layers by type (walls, windows, doors, stairs, dimensions, annotations).
Room Context Select Choose the room type (office, lobby, residence, retail, etc.) from 70+ options to give the AI spatial context.
Ceiling Type Select Flat · Coffered · Beams · Vaulted
North Orientation Compass Rotate the compass to match your drawing orientation so lighting matches the real sun angle.

Sketch to Render

Hand-drawn or digital sketches Perspective auto-detection

Upload a hand-drawn or digital sketch and AVAS will transform it into a finished architectural render. It automatically analyzes the perspective type, drawing quality, and vanishing points to produce an accurate result.

Automatic Sketch Analysis

Upload your sketch and AVAS will automatically detect:

  • Perspective type — 1-point, 2-point, 3-point, isometric, axonometric, or freehand
  • Vanishing points — displayed as overlays on the canvas
  • Line quality — a 0-100 score for how clean your linework is
  • Completeness — how much of the design is drawn in

Generate from Text

No image required Chat-style interface

Don't have a 3D model? Just describe what you want. Type a description of your architectural concept in the prompt bar at the bottom and AVAS will generate a photorealistic image from your words alone.

Example prompt: "A minimalist concrete house on a hillside with floor-to-ceiling glass, surrounded by olive trees, golden hour lighting, Mediterranean setting."

Tips for Better Results

  • Describe materials specifically: "exposed concrete", "aged oak", "weathered steel"
  • Include lighting conditions: "golden hour", "overcast sky", "interior night lighting"
  • Mention surrounding context: "urban street", "forest clearing", "coastal cliff"
  • Specify camera angle: "street-level perspective", "aerial view", "interior shot"
  • All 16 architectural styles and right panel settings apply equally here

Visual Edit

Design Selection-based editing 9 tools

Make targeted AI edits to any part of an image. Select a region using the brush, rectangle, or lasso tool, then choose what to do with it — change materials, adjust lighting, remove elements, add people, replace the sky, and more.

How It Works

  1. Upload the image you want to edit.
  2. Choose a selection tool (rectangle, brush, or lasso) and draw over the area you want to change.
  3. Choose an operation from the left toolbar.
  4. Add a description in the prompt if needed (e.g. "replace with aged oak panels").
  5. Generate.

Operation Tools

🎨 Material — Change any surface finish. Select a wall and replace it with a different material.

💡 Lighting — Modify how light falls on the selected area.

📦 Object — Replace architectural elements (windows, doors, furniture).

🧑 People — Add, reposition, or remove human figures.

☁️ Sky — Change sky condition, clouds, time of day.

🗑️ Remove — Delete unwanted elements; background fills in automatically.

🎚️ Adjust — Fine-tune colors, brightness, and saturation in a region.

↔️ Extend — Expand the image canvas beyond its original boundaries.

🌆 Background — Replace the surroundings while keeping the subject.

Auto-Select: In the "Select" tool, you can type what you want to select (e.g. "all windows") and AVAS will automatically detect and outline those elements for you.

Section Render

Design Architectural sections

Transform a flat architectural section drawing into a rendered visualization with depth, materials, and spatial atmosphere. AVAS detects the program areas visible in the section and renders each zone accordingly.

Area Detection

Click Detect Areas and AVAS will scan the section and list all major spatial zones (living areas, circulation, mechanical, etc.). You can edit, toggle, and add descriptions to each zone before generating.

Multi-Angle

Design Up to 8 views in one generation

Generate up to 8 views of your building from different camera angles all at once — front, back, sides, aerial, and custom angles — delivered as a single organized grid image.

View Configuration

  • View Count — Choose how many angles to generate (typically 4, 6, or 8).
  • Azimuth Range — Set the horizontal sweep range (0° = front, 90° = right, 180° = rear).
  • Elevation Range — Set the vertical tilt (0° = eye level, 45° = elevated, 90° = top-down).
  • Manual Angles — Specify exact angles if needed.
Output: All views are delivered as a single grid image. You can download the complete grid or select individual panels.

Upscale

Enhance Batch up to 20 images Up to 4× resolution

Enlarge images up to 4× their original resolution while maintaining — or improving — quality. AVAS uses AI upscaling to add fine detail rather than simply stretching pixels. Process up to 20 images at once.

How to Batch Upscale

Go to Upscale mode and click + Add Images in the left sidebar.
Select up to 20 images. They'll be added to the queue.
Click Generate to start processing. Images process sequentially.
Download completed images as they finish, or batch download all at the end.
Cost saving: Processing images in a batch costs approximately 30% less than processing them individually.

Note: This mode produces guidance for CAD tracing, not a direct CAD file. The AI identifies what to trace and how, which your team then follows in their CAD software.

Image to 3D

Convert Multi-view input

Reconstruct a 3D model from one or more reference photographs. Upload multiple views of a building (front, side, rear) and AVAS will generate a 3D model suitable for further development.

Input Tips

Full Reference

Render Settings

The right panel in 3D Rendering and CAD to Render modes gives you granular control over every aspect of the output. Here's a full reference.

You don't need to change everything. AVAS's defaults produce excellent results. Only adjust settings when you have a specific look in mind.
📐 Geometry ▾
Edge Mode Select Soft · Medium · Sharp — controls how the AI defines edges and outlines.
Strict Preservation Toggle When on, AVAS stays very close to the uploaded geometry. When off, a Geometry Freedom slider lets the AI make creative modifications.
Detail Level (LOD) Select Low · Medium · High — plus toggles for Ornaments, Moldings, and Trim preservation.
Smoothing Toggle + Slider Smooths curved surfaces. Intensity 0–100%. Option to preserve hard edges above a threshold angle.
Displacement Toggle + Controls Adds texture depth to materials. Strength 0–100%, scale (Fine / Medium / Coarse), material-adaptive mode.
💡 Lighting ▾
Sun Azimuth Slider (0–360°) Horizontal direction of the sun. 0° = north, 90° = east, 180° = south, 270° = west.
Sun Elevation Slider (0–90°) Height of the sun above the horizon. 0° = sunset, 45° = mid-afternoon, 90° = overhead.
Sun Intensity Slider (0–100%) How strong the direct sunlight is.
Color Temperature Slider Warm (3000K) to cool (8000K). Affects the overall color cast of the lighting.
Shadow Intensity Slider (0–100%) How dark the shadows are. Lower values produce softer, more diffused shadows.
Ambient Occlusion Slider (0–100%) Darkens corners and crevices for more grounded depth. Essential for realistic results.
Mood Presets Buttons Golden Hour · Soft Daylight · Overcast · Moody · Night · Studio
📷 Camera ▾
Lens Slider 20mm (ultra-wide) to 100mm (telephoto). 35–50mm = natural human vision.
Auto-Correct Toggle Fixes converging verticals for more natural-looking architectural photography.
Depth of Field Toggle + Controls Blurs foreground and background for a photography look. Aperture: f/1.4 to f/16. Focus distance: 0–100m.
🧱 Materials ▾
Material Emphasis 10 Sliders Individual emphasis controls for: Concrete, Wood, Metal, Glass, Stone, Brick, Tile, Fabric, Paint, Flooring. Higher values = that material is more prominent.
Reflectivity Slider (0–100%) Global reflectivity of all surfaces.
Roughness Slider (0–100%) Global surface roughness — lower = more polished, higher = matte.
Weathering Toggle + Slider Adds wear, patina, and aging effects to materials. Great for making renders look lived-in.
🌫️ Atmosphere ▾
Mood Text Free-text mood description. Example: "dreamy", "melancholic", "vibrant urban morning".
Fog Toggle + Slider Adds atmospheric haze. Density 0–100%.
Bloom Toggle + Slider Adds a soft glow around bright light sources. Intensity 0–100%.
Temperature Slider (-50 to +50) Shifts the overall image from cool/blue (-50) to warm/amber (+50).
🌳 Scenery ▾
People Toggle + Count Add pedestrians and figures. Control the count (0–50+).
Trees Toggle + Count Add trees and greenery. Control the count (0–100+).
Cars Toggle + Count Add vehicles. Control the count (0–20+).
🖨️ Output Format ▾
Resolution Select 720p · 1080p · 2K · 4K
Aspect Ratio Select 16:9 · 4:3 · 3:2 · 1:1 · 21:9 · 9:16 · 3:4 · 4:5 · 5:4 · 2:3
Quality Select Draft (fast) · Preview · Production · Ultra (best quality)

Best Practices

Tips & Tricks

🎯
Start with defaults
AVAS's default settings are carefully tuned. Unless you need something specific, generate with defaults first — the results are often excellent without any adjustment.
🔬
Use Problem Area Analysis
In 3D Rendering mode, always run the Problem Area Analysis for complex models. It only takes a few seconds and significantly improves output for detailed geometry.
📐
Clean inputs = better outputs
A clear, well-lit screenshot of your 3D model will produce better renders than a blurry or low-contrast input. Take the time to export a clean viewport capture.
📦
Batch to save credits
For upscaling and PDF compression, always use batch mode. You'll save approximately 30% of credits compared to processing files one by one.
💾
Use the History panel
Every generation is saved in the History tab. You can always go back and download an earlier version — you never permanently lose a generation within a session.
🎨
Combine modes
Generate a render in 3D Rendering mode, then use Visual Edit to fine-tune the sky, people, or materials. These modes work beautifully in sequence.
⚡
Draft mode for quick tests
When exploring settings, use "Draft" quality in the right panel. It generates faster and costs fewer credits — save "Production" or "Ultra" for your final deliverable.
🌐
Background reference anchors the render
In 3D Rendering mode, uploading a background reference photo of the real site dramatically improves how well the building fits its context in the final render.
🔒
Lock the seed for video
In Video mode, lock the seed number after a generation you like. This lets you re-generate with different quality settings while keeping the same camera movement.

Common Questions

Why is my render not matching my model closely?
Try setting Rendering Mode to "Strict Realism" and enabling "Strict Preservation" in the Geometry settings. Also run Problem Area Analysis to identify challenging zones.

The generation seems to take a long time — is this normal?
Generation time depends on resolution and quality settings. 4K Ultra can take 30–60 seconds. Try "Draft" mode for faster exploration, and switch to "Production" or "Ultra" for final outputs.

Can I edit the AI prompt manually?
Yes — the Prompt tab in the bottom panel shows the full prompt AVAS generated from your settings. You can edit it freely. Click "Revert" to restore the auto-generated prompt.

How do I share a result with my team?
Download the image using the Download button in the top-right, and share it via your usual channels (email, Slack, Teams, etc.). AVAS does not have a built-in sharing feature at this time.

I ran out of credits — what now?
You can purchase a credit top-up (500 or 2,000 credits) from your Billing settings without waiting for your monthly reset. Contact your administrator if you're on a Studio plan.
